# Tendo

> *"Tendo"* is Luganda for **praise / commendation** — the word parents say when the PLE results come back.

A focused, school-sold web platform that helps Ugandan upper-primary students master **PLE Mathematics** through structured notes, auto-graded quizzes, and real past papers.

## Why Tendo exists

Ugandan parents spend **UGX 450,000 – 2,500,000 per term** on private primary school, largely chasing strong PLE results. The PLE Math syllabus is **finite and knowable** (6 themes, 11 topics, 40 sub-topics) and past papers are freely available — yet no clean, locally-built study product packages this for schools to offer parents at scale.

Tendo is that product.

## Who it's for

- **Students** (P6 & P7): a calm, focused place to revise topic by topic and try real past papers.
- **Schools** (private primary, urban + semi-urban): a value-add for parents that signals exam seriousness without adding teacher load.
- **Parents** (paying indirectly through school): visibility into what their child has practiced.

## What's in v0

- Topic notes (markdown-based, clean typography)
- Auto-graded multiple-choice quizzes (5–10 questions per topic)
- Past PLE papers (browseable, attemptable, marked)
- Progress saved locally (no login required in v0)
