# Study Lesson Structure v2

*Date: 2026-06-30*
*Purpose: replace summary-like read sections with more teachable, child-friendly, curriculum-aligned lesson content.*

---

## Why this new structure exists

The earlier topic-note structure was good enough for a compact demo and quick revision flow, but it is too compressed for a real self-study product.

The founder's concern is correct:
- some Read sections still feel like summaries
- some still sound too robotic
- some heavy topics are trying to fit too much into one page

So this v2 structure is designed for **study**, not just revision.

---

## Core writing principles

## 1. Teach, do not just summarise
The learner should feel guided from one idea to the next.
Do not assume understanding after one paragraph.
Build the concept in steps.

## 2. Write like a patient upper-primary teacher
The student is probably 12 or 13.
They may be reading on a phone.
English may not be their first language.
Use plain, warm, direct language.

## 3. Stay strictly inside the NCDC scope
Every explanation, example, and task must be clearly aligned to the P7 Mathematics curriculum.
Do not add fancy extensions just because they are mathematically interesting.

## 4. Use small teaching chunks
A child should be able to pause after one idea and still feel they learned something.
Avoid giant walls of explanation.

## 5. Use examples that actually teach
Examples should not just prove the answer.
They should show the method and the thinking.

---

## Recommended lesson flow

### 1. What you will learn
Short, concrete objectives.
Use learner-friendly verbs:
- read
- find
- compare
- change
- solve
- check

### 2. Start here
A short opening that tells the learner what the topic is in simple language.
This should feel like the teacher helping the learner settle into the lesson.

### 3. Learn it step by step
Break the core idea into 2 to 5 short sub-ideas.
Each sub-idea should feel digestible.
Do not overload a single paragraph.

### 4. Worked example
At least one fully explained example.
For bigger topics, two examples may be better.
The steps should sound like a teacher walking through the work.

### 5. Watch out
Use this for common mistakes.
The tone should be protective and practical, not scolding.
Example: "Many pupils forget..." or "Be careful not to..."

### 6. Try one
One short practice task inside the Read section.
The answer and explanation should help the learner check understanding immediately.

### 7. What to remember
Short recap bullets with the key rules or ideas.
These should be highly scannable.

---

## Tone guidelines

### Prefer this kind of tone
- "Let us start with..."
- "First, notice that..."
- "Now do the same thing here..."
- "Be careful not to..."
- "If that looks confusing, use this quick rule..."

### Avoid this kind of tone
- "In this lesson, we shall explore..."
- "The learner is expected to..."
- "This topic is important because..." repeated too mechanically
- stiff textbook-style wording
- overly polished AI-sounding transitions

---

## Structural guidance for larger topics

Some topics should eventually become sub-lessons instead of one compressed page.

Examples:
- Fractions
- Percentages
- Time
- Area and volume applications
- Data handling

For now, if a full route split is not yet built, still write the content as if it has clear internal sub-sections.
That makes the later UI split easier.

---

## NCDC alignment rule

Before finalising a lesson, check:
1. Is this concept in the P7 Math curriculum map?
2. Is the example within upper-primary level?
3. Would a teacher say this still belongs inside the syllabus?
4. Is the explanation helping the learner understand, not just memorise?

If the answer to any of these is no, revise before shipping.
