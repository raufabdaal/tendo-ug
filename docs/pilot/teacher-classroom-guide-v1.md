# Tendo Teacher Classroom Guide v1

*For P7 pilot use at Trainup a Child Uganda or similar schools*

## What Tendo is for

Tendo is a simple PLE study and practice tool for P7 learners.

It helps a teacher:

- introduce or revise a topic quickly
- let learners read structured notes
- use diagrams and PLE tips for explanation
- give short quizzes
- generate printable worksheets
- see what learners practise on the device

It is not meant to replace the teacher. It is meant to make revision, practice and worksheet preparation faster.

---

## Best classroom use cases

### 1. Five-minute topic introduction

Use this when starting a lesson or revision period.

Steps:

1. Open the subject.
2. Pick the topic.
3. Read the **Big idea** aloud.
4. Ask learners what key words they already know.
5. Use the **See it** diagram to explain the topic visually.
6. End with the **PLE tip**.

Good for:

- Maths formulas
- Science systems/processes
- Social Studies maps/timelines
- English writing formats

---

### 2. Learner self-study

Use this when learners have shared devices, a computer lab, or teacher-guided phone/tablet access.

Steps:

1. Assign one topic.
2. Ask learners to read:
   - Big idea
   - Key words
   - Learn it in parts
   - PLE tip
3. Learners attempt **Try this**.
4. Learners take the quiz or practice mode.
5. Teacher asks 2–3 learners to explain what they learnt.

---

### 3. Quick revision before a test

Steps:

1. Choose 2–3 weak topics.
2. Use each topic's **Quick recap**.
3. Let learners answer the **Try this** question.
4. Generate a worksheet with 10–20 questions.
5. Mark together or use the answer key.

---

### 4. Worksheet preparation

Steps:

1. Open `/teacher/worksheet`.
2. Choose subject:
   - Maths only
   - English only
   - Science only
   - Social Studies only
   - Mixed
3. Select topics.
4. Add class, term/week, time allowed and marks per question.
5. Choose number of questions.
6. Generate, print or copy.
7. Keep the answer key separate from the learner worksheet.

## Suggested weekly pilot routine

### Option A: One subject focus per day

- Monday: Mathematics
- Tuesday: English
- Wednesday: Integrated Science
- Thursday: Social Studies
- Friday: Mixed revision worksheet

### Option B: Weak-topic revision

Each week, teacher selects:

- 2 weak Maths topics
- 1 weak English writing topic
- 1 weak Science topic
- 1 weak SST topic

Learners study and practise only those topics.

---

## What teachers should observe

During the pilot, teachers should watch for:

- Do learners understand the **Big idea**?
- Are the **Key words** clear?
- Do diagrams help, or confuse learners?
- Are PLE tips useful?
- Are quizzes too easy, too hard or about right?
- Are worksheet questions printable and classroom-ready?
- Which topics need more examples?

---

## Known limitations to explain honestly

- Progress is saved on the device, not on a school-wide login yet.
- Teacher dashboard is useful for demo/local tracking, but full multi-device reporting needs a future backend.
- Maths central bank currently has 195 questions across 13 bank topics, while all 38 Maths pages still have topic quizzes.
- Videos are mostly marked as coming soon.

---

## Recommended teacher feedback question

After using Tendo, ask:

> If you had this every week, what would make it more useful for your P7 class?

This question is better than asking only whether they “like” the product.
