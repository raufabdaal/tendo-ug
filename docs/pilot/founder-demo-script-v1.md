# Founder Demo Script v1

## Goal

Help the founder show Tendo clearly to a school leader or P7 teacher in 5–10 minutes.

Tone: practical, honest, teacher-respecting.

---

## 30-second opening

> Tendo is a simple PLE study and practice tool for upper primary learners. Right now it focuses on P7 Mathematics, English, Integrated Science and Social Studies. The goal is not to replace teachers. The goal is to make revision, practice and worksheet preparation easier for teachers and more structured for learners.

---

## Demo flow

### 1. Start on the home page

Say:

> The learner or teacher starts by choosing a subject. There is no login, so it is quick to open in class or on a shared device.

Show:

- Mathematics
- English
- Integrated Science
- Social Studies
- Teacher dashboard
- Worksheet generator

---

### 2. Open a Maths topic

Recommended topic:

- Venn diagrams
- Fractions
- Area
- Money

Say:

> Each topic is now structured like a short lesson, not just notes. The learner starts with the Big idea, then key words, then the topic is broken into smaller parts.

Point out:

- Big idea
- Key words
- Learn it in parts
- Visual brief
- See it diagram
- PLE tip
- Worked example
- Try this

Say:

> The PLE tip is important because many learners know the content but lose marks through common mistakes.

---

### 3. Open an English topic

Recommended topic:

- Letter writing

Say:

> English is handled differently because it needs writing formats, tone and practice. For example, letter writing shows formal and informal structure, then gives guided writing practice.

Point out:

- Formal vs informal explanation
- Guided practice
- Marking checklist
- Model opening

---

### 4. Open a Science topic

Recommended topic:

- Electricity and magnetism
- Excretory system

Say:

> Science topics use key words, process explanations, safety notes and diagrams. This helps learners revise systems and experiments in a more visual way.

Point out:

- circuit/system diagram
- safety-focused PLE tip
- guided experiment/application task

---

### 5. Open a Social Studies topic

Recommended topic:

- Location of Africa
- Climate of Africa

Say:

> Social Studies topics use maps, timelines, comparison cards and cause-effect thinking. This is useful because SST learners often need to explain, not just memorise.

Point out:

- map/visual
- key vocabulary
- guided map task

---

### 6. Show worksheet generator

Say:

> For teachers, this is one of the most useful parts. A teacher can choose subject, topics, number of questions, marks and time, then print or copy a worksheet with an answer key.

Point out:

- subject filters
- question-bank pool
- metadata fields
- quick length buttons
- answer key option

Say:

> This can reduce preparation time for revision work.

---

## Honest limitations

Say this before they ask:

> This is still an early pilot version. Progress is saved on the device, not yet on a full school backend. Most videos are still coming soon. What we want first is teacher feedback on whether the content, diagrams, quizzes and worksheets are useful in real P7 classes.

This honesty builds trust.

---

## Questions to ask the teacher

After demo, ask:

1. Would this help you revise P7 topics faster?
2. Which subject is most useful first?
3. Would you use the worksheet generator?
4. Are the explanations at the right level for your learners?
5. Which diagrams or topics need improvement?
6. What would make this useful every week?

---

## Strong closing

> We are not trying to guess what schools need from far away. We want to test this with real P7 teachers, improve it based on your feedback, and make it useful for everyday revision and PLE preparation.

---

## If they ask about payment

Say:

> For now, the goal is feedback and proof of usefulness. If teachers find it genuinely useful, then we can discuss a simple school-friendly model later.

---

## If they ask about other classes

Say:

> The current product is P7-first because of PLE pressure. P6 is the natural next step after we confirm the P7 workflow with teachers.
