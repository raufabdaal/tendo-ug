# P7 Social Studies deepening pass — 2026-07-01

## Verdict

P7 Social Studies has been deepened beyond its earlier complete-enough state. It now has stronger map, geography, history, development and organisation-practice depth across all 10 official NCDC topics.

Social Studies has fewer official top-level topics than Mathematics because the NCDC SST outline is organised under one broad theme: **Living Together in Africa**. The correct product-depth standard is not artificially increasing topic pages; it is giving each topic enough map/source/case-study/application practice for real PLE preparation.

---

## What changed

### 1. Guided SST tasks added for all 10 topics
Social Studies now has guided tasks for:

1. Location of Africa
   - map task: locating Africa
2. Physical Features of Africa
   - case study: how physical features help people
3. Climate of Africa
   - climate reasoning task: farming and settlement
4. Vegetation of Africa
   - vegetation comparison task
5. People, Ethnic Groups and Settlement Patterns
   - migration task: push and pull factors
6. Foreign Influence in Africa
   - history source task: foreign groups in Africa
7. Nationalism and the Road to Independence
   - nationalist profile task
8. Post-Independence Africa
   - problem-solution task
9. Economic Developments in Africa
   - country case study: resources and development
10. Major World Organisations
   - organisation matching task

Each guided task includes:
- prompt
- planning steps
- checklist
- model opening

### 2. SST practice-bank depth increased
Social Studies multiple-choice practice was expanded from 12 questions per topic to 20 questions per topic.

Current Social Studies practice depth:
- 10 topics
- 20 questions per topic
- 200 Social Studies practice questions total

## Why this matters

SST exam readiness depends heavily on:
- map interpretation
- explaining relationships between people and environment
- history cause/effect
- country case studies
- organisation-function matching
- responsible citizenship
- development problem-solving

The guided tasks target those higher-value skills without inventing non-official topics.
