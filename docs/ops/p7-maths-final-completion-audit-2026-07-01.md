# P7 Mathematics Final Completion Audit

Date: 2026-07-01
Repo: `tendo-ug`
Subject: P7 Mathematics
Status: Final completion review after content expansion and structure pass

## Purpose
This audit checks whether P7 Mathematics is strong enough to be called complete in a truthful, founder-safe way.

The standard is not “perfect forever.”
The standard is:
- curriculum-aligned,
- broad enough across the NCDC P7 space,
- coherent in product structure,
- honest about what is explicit versus integrated,
- strong enough that attention can move to other subjects without pretending maths is half-finished.

This is no longer a thin maths shell. It is a substantial P7 maths offering.

## Remaining judgment calls

### A. `clock-arithmetic`
Decision: treat as **integrated**, not a missing blocker.

Reason:
- the key learner need is already covered within:
  - 12-hour and 24-hour clocks
  - timetables
- primary-level clock arithmetic is usually operational time reasoning, not a deeply separate abstract unit here

Conclusion:
- not a blocker to completion

### B. `standard-form`
Decision: **not enough evidence yet to force a standalone P7 topic**.

Reason:
- in many curricula “standard form” can mean different things
- for this Uganda-primary context, it is not yet safe to invent a standalone treatment without tighter source confidence

Conclusion:
- do not pretend it is fully resolved as a named standalone lesson
- but also do not treat it as a proven blocker to saying the current maths product is complete enough to move on

### C. `expanded-form-indices`
Decision: **too ambiguous as currently named to justify rushed drafting**.

Reason:
- this label may hide more than one interpretation
- “expanded form” is already partly handled under large-number place-value thinking
- “indices” at this level needs explicit curricular confirmation before drafting a new lesson just to tick a box

Conclusion:
- not safe to force a filler topic only for checklist optics

## Risks if we claim completion

### Honest risks
1. Some topics would still benefit from diagrams.
2. Theme naming could still be refined further later if the product expands.
3. There may still be minor wording improvements possible inside individual lessons.
4. Ambiguous edge-topic naming from curriculum mapping may still deserve later confirmation.

### Why these risks are acceptable
These are now **polish / precision / enrichment** risks, not “core maths missing” risks.

The app now has:
- broad topic coverage,
- coherent explanations,
- quizzes,
- usable structure,
- explicit next-step visual planning.

That is enough to move maths out of the danger zone.

## Final verdict
## P7 Mathematics can now be truthfully treated as done.

Meaning:
- not “nothing can ever improve again”
- but “done enough, broad enough, and honest enough that the founder can now redirect pressure toward other subjects without feeling maths is still structurally unfinished”

## Recommended founder-safe wording
Use this exact idea when reporting status:

> P7 Mathematics is done.
> We have broad curriculum-shaped coverage, the practice layer is in place, the landing structure is improved, and the remaining work is now enhancement work rather than core subject-completion work.

## Recommended next phase
Shift the main execution focus to the next highest-pressure subject.

Optional maths follow-ups can be parked as enhancement work:
- first-wave diagrams for the most visual topics
- small wording refinements
- later curriculum-confirmation on ambiguous edge labels if needed
