# Value Proposition — Tendo

*The wedge, the math, the language to use. Read before any sales conversation.*

---

## The one-liner

> **Tendo is a PLE Mathematics revision platform that your school offers parents, and that quietly lifts your division-one rate.**

(Use this verbatim when a head teacher asks "so what is it?")

## The 30-second pitch

> *"You already know your school's reputation rides on PLE results. You also know parents are paying for extra coaching outside school. Tendo is a clean web app, branded to your school, that gives every P7 student a structured way to revise the entire UNEB Math syllabus, plus past papers, plus auto-marked quizzes. Schools pay us a small per-student fee per term. You can charge parents whatever you like on top. We handle the technology; you keep the relationship and the margin."*

## The 5-minute pitch (structure)

1. **The world the head teacher lives in** — PLE league tables, division-one targets, parents asking "what extra are you doing for my child?"
2. **What's NOT working today** — printed past-paper booklets the student loses; weekend coaching that exhausts the teachers; YouTube videos from Nigerian or Kenyan teachers using a different syllabus.
3. **Tendo's wedge** — Ugandan, PLE-aligned, school-branded, on any phone, $0 setup for the school.
4. **What the parent sees** — "Your school has partnered with Tendo to give your child structured PLE revision."
5. **What the school sees** — usage report each term, no IT burden, set-and-forget.
6. **The ask** — pilot one P7 class this term, free. Decide whether to roll out next term.

## Why this works (the underlying logic)

### 1. The PLE Math syllabus is a closed system

NCDC defines it: **6 themes, 11 topics, 40 sub-topics.** This hasn't materially changed in 14 years. There is a *correct* answer to "what should a P7 student practice for PLE Math?" and we have it.

### 2. The buyer (school) and the user (student) are aligned

The school wins when the student wins. Unlike B2C edutech, where engagement and learning often diverge, here both parties want the same outcome: better PLE marks.

### 3. The school owns the parent relationship — and that's a feature, not a bug

We never have to acquire a parent. We never have to convince a parent we are trustworthy. The school does both, because they already are trusted.

### 4. The economics are absurdly in our favor

See `pricing.md`, but the short version:

| | Current state | With Tendo |
|---|---|---|
| Parent spend per term (private school) | UGX 450,000 – 2,500,000 | Same |
| Add-on for "PLE Revision Programme" | UGX 30,000 – 100,000 (paid to weekend coach) | UGX 15,000 – 30,000 (paid to school, includes Tendo) |
| Tendo's cut | — | UGX 5,000 – 10,000 per student per term |
| School's cut | — | UGX 10,000 – 20,000 per student per term |

A school of **120 P7 students** generates the school **UGX 1.2M – 2.4M per term** in new margin while raising their PLE prep credibility. That same school costs us roughly **UGX 0** to serve once content is built.

## The objection we cannot answer with words

> *"How do I know it actually works?"*

We don't yet. The honest answer in the first 1–3 sales conversations is: **"You don't. That's why the first term is free for one P7 class. We will give you a usage report. Your teachers can compare those students' end-of-term scores to the other stream."**

This converts "I'm not sure" into "let's try it." See `objections.md` for more.

## The features that turn "maybe" into "yes"

After Phase 4 these are doing most of the heavy lifting in head-teacher meetings:

1. **Past PLE papers with teacher mode.** Same content, two interfaces. Students attempt, teachers browse-by-topic and print worksheets. Saves the average P7 teacher ~1 hour of question-prep per week. Three papers in v0 (2018, 2019, 2020); more in pipeline.
2. **Worksheet generator.** Mix any topics, pick any length, pick difficulty, generate. Print for class or copy to WhatsApp. **This is the feature teachers ask to use a second time before they finish the demo.** Pool: ~200 questions across all topics, reshuffled every time so no two worksheets are the same.
3. **Endless Practice mode for students.** Every topic has a "Start practice" button. Different questions every time, with a streak counter and instant feedback. The student never runs out of practice.
4. **Watch · Read** tabs on every topic (Listen icon inside the Read view). Video coming for high-stakes topics in Phase 5; text + audio narration works today on any device.
5. **Class dashboard with bar charts + student drill-down.** Class-level view shows performance by topic at a glance — red bars flag topics that need re-teaching. Click any student to see their individual progress. One-click "Copy report for parents."
6. **Teacher Fellowship.** Every question has a "report a problem" link. Teachers who contribute 5+ accepted reviews per term become public Tendo Teacher Fellows. Converts "teachers feel replaced" into "teachers gain recognition." See `teacher-contribution-strategy.md`.

## What we are NOT

- We are not a tutor. There is no human in the loop for the student.
- We are not a textbook replacement. We complement what the teacher does.
- We are not "AI-powered learning" in our pitch. The AI is in our kitchen, not on our menu. Head teachers want exam results, not buzzwords.
- We are not a parent-app. The parent's view of Tendo is the school's WhatsApp message and the report the teacher exports.

## The story we tell at the end

> *"Five years from now, when a parent asks a head teacher 'what makes your school PLE-strong?', we want the honest answer to include Tendo. Not as the headline. As a quiet, dependable part of how your school does revision."*
