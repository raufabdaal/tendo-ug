# Sales Objections — Tendo Cheat Sheet

*The 10 most likely things a head teacher will say, and what to say back. Practice these out loud before any meeting.*

---

## 1. "How do we know it actually improves PLE results?"

**Bad answer:** *"Our system uses AI-driven personalized learning paths..."*

**Good answer:**
> *"You don't yet. Nobody can promise results from a tool. What I can promise is that the entire UNEB Math syllabus is structured into the app, every quiz has worked explanations, and your teachers will see in the termly report which students are actually revising. If a P7 student does 30 topics on Tendo in a term, their PLE chances aren't worse — that's the floor. The first term is free precisely so you can see for yourself before paying."*

---

## 2. "Our students don't all have phones."

**Good answer:**
> *"True for some. Tendo runs on any phone the parent already has — feature phones won't work, but a basic UGX 200,000 Android does. For students without devices at home, we recommend a 30-minute slot in the school computer lab once a week, or shared parent phones in the evening. We're not trying to be the only revision channel. We're trying to be the one that works after the school day ends."*

---

## 3. "Don't we just need more past papers, not a website?"

**Good answer:**
> *"Past papers are part of Tendo — we have them. But a stack of paper past papers has three problems: students lose them, they're not marked, and a student doesn't know WHICH topic they're weak in. Tendo tells you 'this child is strong on Fractions but failing Volume.' Then your teacher knows where to focus the Saturday review."*

---

## 4. "What about data — the students will use up all their MTN bundles."

**Good answer:**
> *"Each topic page is about the size of a WhatsApp photo. A student can revise 30 topics on less than 50 MB. We deliberately have no videos and no autoplay anything. We also cache the topic so once it's opened, it works offline."*

---

## 5. "We already have textbooks. Why pay for this on top?"

**Good answer:**
> *"Tendo is not a replacement for the textbook. It's the practice layer. The textbook teaches; Tendo drills. The textbook has 20 practice questions per chapter; Tendo has 7 questions per sub-topic, with explanations, that the student can repeat any night. Different tools for different jobs."*

---

## 6. "What if my teachers feel replaced?"

**Good answer:**
> *"They won't, and we should be careful they don't feel that way. Tendo is for evenings and weekends — the time when your teachers aren't there. In school hours, your teachers still teach. We actually built a teacher mode specifically so the tool works FOR them: every past PLE paper has a Browse-by-topic view that lets a teacher pick the questions they want and print a worksheet in two clicks. Their class prep gets shorter, not their job."*
>
> *"And one more thing — our content evolves with feedback from your teachers. Every question has a 'report a problem' link. If a teacher at your school contributes five or more accepted reviews in a term, they become a Tendo Teacher Fellow with a certificate. Your teachers gain visibility, not lose it."*

*(See `teacher-contribution-strategy.md` for the full Fellowship framing. We don't yet have schools using this in production. When asked who, redirect: "Once we have a paid school, I'll put you in touch with them directly.")*

---

## 7. "Why should I trust you / who built this?"

**Good answer (the founder version):**
> *"My name is [founder]. I built [previous product / context]. Tendo is a focused project, not a venture-funded startup trying to disrupt anything. We chose schools as our customer specifically because we don't want to chase parents on Facebook or burn through marketing money. We want to build a tool that two or three schools love, then five more, then ten. If we ever stop being useful to your school, you stop paying. That's the model."*

---

## 8. "Is the content correct? Is it aligned with the Ugandan syllabus?"

**Good answer:**
> *"Yes. Every topic in Tendo maps to a specific sub-topic in the NCDC P7 Mathematics curriculum — same 6 themes, 11 topics, 40 sub-topics that UNEB examines. Past papers are direct from UNEB. Every quiz question is reviewed by a Ugandan-trained teacher before it goes live. Show me a question right now that looks off and I'll fix it within 48 hours."*

*(Have the curriculum tree printed on the back of the deck. Pull it out here.)*

---

## 9. "What's the catch?"

**Good answer:**
> *"The catch is that this is early. We're a small team, the app does Math only for now (English, Science, and SST come next year), and you'd be one of our first schools — which means you get more attention from us, but also that we're still learning. If you want a polished, big-brand product with 50 features, we are not it. If you want something simple that works, that's exactly what we are."*

---

## 10. "Send me the contract and let me think about it."

**Good answer:**
> *"Of course. To keep this moving, can I propose a specific next step? I'll email you the one-page MoU today. If you can sign or come back with questions by [date 7 days out], we can have your school live before the start of [next month]. I'll follow up by WhatsApp on day 5 with a single 'yes/no/needs more time' message."*

*(The follow-up commitment is the close. Make it explicit. Schedule the WhatsApp message in your calendar before you leave their office.)*

---

## Two questions to ask THEM (use these to diagnose, not pitch)

- *"What does your school currently do for P7 revision, beyond regular classroom hours?"*
- *"If we sat here this time next year and you said 'Tendo really worked for us,' what would have to be true?"*

The second question is gold. It tells you what success looks like to *this* head teacher, in their words, which you can then quote back in every follow-up.

---

## Things you should NEVER say in a sales meeting

- ❌ "Disrupt" anything
- ❌ "Game-changer" / "revolutionary"
- ❌ "Powered by AI" (technical reality, not a selling point here)
- ❌ "Scalable" (founder word, not a customer word)
- ❌ Any comparison that disparages another Ugandan edutech
- ❌ Any number you can't back up if asked
- ❌ "We can definitely build that" (until you've checked the roadmap)
