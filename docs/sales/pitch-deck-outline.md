# Pitch Deck Outline — Tendo for Schools

*A 9-slide deck. 15 minutes including questions. Designed for a head teacher's office, on a laptop, possibly without internet. So: PDF export, no embedded videos.*

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Tendo**
*PLE Mathematics revision, built for Ugandan upper-primary.*

Sub-line: *"A platform your school offers parents."*

Bottom: your name + contact.

**You say:** *"Thanks for the 15 minutes. I'm going to show you what Tendo is, why we built it for schools like yours, and how a pilot would work this term. Stop me anytime."*

---

## Slide 2 — The pressure you live under

A photo-free slide. Three statements stacked.

> **Your PLE division-one rate is the number every parent asks about.**
>
> **Coaching, extra books, weekend revision — all paid for by parents, all outside your control.**
>
> **And the resources online are mostly Kenyan, Nigerian, or American — wrong syllabus.**

**You say:** *"I'm not telling you anything you don't know. I'm just naming the gap."*

---

## Slide 3 — The PLE Math syllabus is actually finite

A visual: 6 themes → 11 topics → 40 sub-topics. A tree diagram.

**You say:** *"This is the entire UNEB Mathematics syllabus for P7. If a student practices every sub-topic with real questions, they're prepared. We built Tendo around exactly this tree."*

This slide establishes credibility — you know the syllabus, not just the buzzwords.

---

## Slide 4 — What students see (Tendo in action)

Three screenshots side-by-side:
- Topic list
- A topic page with notes
- A quiz screen with a correct/incorrect explanation

**You say:** *"They open it on any phone. Pick a topic. Read 2 minutes of notes. Take a 7-question quiz. Get immediate feedback with explanations. Their progress saves on the device."*

---

## Slide 5 — What you get as the school

Four bullets:

- 🏫 **Branded for your school.** Subdomain like `tendo-stmarys.ug`.
- 📊 **Termly usage report.** Which students are revising, which topics, what scores.
- 🔧 **Zero IT burden.** It's a website. No installation. No server. No support tickets.
- 👨‍🏫 **Your teachers can assign topics in class.** "Tonight, do the Fractions topic on Tendo." Self-marking.

**You say:** *"We don't add to your teachers' load. We give them a tool to make their existing revision more effective."*

---

## Slide 6 — What parents see

A mock parent-facing message (the kind the school would send on WhatsApp):

> *"Dear parent, our school has partnered with Tendo to give every P7 student structured PLE Mathematics revision at home. Your child can log in on any phone using the school code. The first term is included in the new revision package fee of UGX 25,000."*

**You say:** *"You decide what to charge. You keep the relationship with the parent. We just power the back-end."*

## Slide 8 — The pilot

A simple proposal:

> **Term 1 pilot — free**
>
> - One P7 class (your choice)
> - We set you up on `tendo-<yourschool>.ug` in one week
> - Termly usage report
> - End of term: you decide whether to roll out school-wide next term

**You say:** *"No invoice this term. No contract beyond a one-page MoU. If at the end of the term you don't see value, we shake hands and part. If you do, we talk Term 2 pricing."*

## Slide 9 — What now

Three buttons (as text — this is a printable deck):

- ✅ **Yes, let's pilot.** Sign the one-page MoU, give us your school name + logo, we go live next week.

## Tone reminders (for you, the seller)

- Don't pitch. Diagnose, then offer.
- Don't say "AI." Say "structured revision."
- Don't say "platform." Say "tool" or "programme."
- Don't say "users." Say "students" and "parents" and "your school."
- Don't say "we will." Say "you will see..."
- When they ask the price, say it cleanly and shut up. The first one to talk loses.
